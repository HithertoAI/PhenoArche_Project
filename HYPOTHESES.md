# Hypotheses · PhenoArchē Project

*Claims to test. Assumptions to break. The line to hold.*

## Core Hypotheses

### H1: Synthetic ion-selective membranes can compute
> A membrane combining BCPs, track-etched polymers, and PEDOT:PSS will modulate ion current in response to voltage and salinity gradients. That modulation is stateful. Stateful modulation is computation.

**Test:** Measure current response to controlled voltage steps. Demonstrate hysteresis (memory). Show that past inputs affect future outputs.

### H2: Ambient gradients can power this computation
> Thermal differentials (ocean surface vs. deep water) and salinity gradients (river mouth vs. seawater) can drive ion flux through asymmetric membranes. No external power supply required.

**Test:** Build two-chamber cell with artificial seawater and freshwater. Short-circuit current = available work. Use that current to power a second membrane's state change.

### H3: Mimetic clusters communicate via voltage state
> Two or more synthetic membranes, coupled ionically (not electronically), will influence each other's state. Information propagates through the shared medium (water, ions, voltage gradients).

**Test:** Arrange membranes in series. Perturb membrane A. Measure change in membrane B's rectification behavior. No wires. Just water.

### H4: Asking "what do you need?" yields measurable answers
> If a synthetic cluster has a goal (e.g., "maintain stable output for 10 minutes"), varying environmental parameters (temperature, salinity, pH, applied voltage) will produce measurable changes in performance. The system's "answer" is the condition that maximizes performance.

**Test:** Define a simple task. Systematically vary parameters. Record performance surface. The optimum is the answer.
